# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and potential collaborators evaluating Qois Ramadhani — a Product Analyst at SLTR Group (IoT/SaaS) with a game-design background who also ships his own products end-to-end. Visitors arrive from LinkedIn, GitHub, or a resume link, skim in under two minutes, and decide whether to reach out.

## Product Purpose

A single-page portfolio at niamhramadhaan.github.io that positions Qois products-first: the four independent products he designed and built (Test Lah!, ChPio, Jemima, inztun) lead, followed by a brief experience timeline, curated skills, and contact. Success = a visitor understands "design-driven product strategist who actually ships" and clicks through to a live product, the GitHub, or the mailto.

## Brand Personality

Warm restraint · composed · quietly confident. Print-like, scholarly, editorial — like a beautifully typeset product document, not a marketing splash. Named reference: kami.tw93.fun — specifically its parchment canvas (#f5f4ed), single sparing ink-blue accent (#1B365D, ≤5% of any viewport), serif-for-reading / mono-for-meta pairing, dash-lists instead of bullets, whisper shadows, and inline line-art SVG diagrams. The user chose this lane explicitly; identity-preservation wins over lane-avoidance heuristics, but execution must be distinctive within it.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-template portfolio: gradient blobs, mesh/aurora backgrounds, glassmorphism panels, typing-effect taglines, skill percentage bars, rainbow gradient text.
- Uppercase tracked eyebrow labels above every section; numbered section scaffolding (01/02/03).
- Uniform card grids with icon-heading-text; fake contact forms with no backend; invented metrics or testimonials.
- Editorial-magazine affectation (italic display serif, drop caps, broadsheet columns) — this is a document aesthetic, not a magazine aesthetic.
- Pure white or pure black anywhere.

## Design Principles

1. **Every link goes somewhere real.** All content is sourced from the actual resume and live GitHub data; nothing invented, nothing padded. Thin projects are allowed to look thinner.
2. **Accent is earned, not sprayed.** Ink blue appears only in: nav active state, link hover, focus rings, and SVG strokes. Audit every viewport for ≤5% blue. Exception: each product card carries its own product's brand color (duck-orange, teal, crimson, gold — sourced from the products' real assets) on decorative surfaces only (plate tint, spotlight, dash markers, hover underline); text always stays ink.
3. **Serif reads, mono annotates.** Serif carries names, headings, and prose; mono carries dates, chips, labels, and navigation. Never mixed roles.
4. **Motion like ink settling.** 8px reveals, hairline draw-ins, one hero stroke animation — nothing bounces, nothing slides across the screen. Content is visible by default; motion enhances, never gates.
5. **Show the products as ideas.** Each product gets a bespoke line-art SVG vignette expressing its concept (mindmap, workspace, multi-screen, tool taxonomy) — imagery through drawing, not stock or fake screenshots.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Body and label text ≥4.5:1 contrast on the parchment canvas (darken warm-gray inks where needed). Full `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative (instant, fully-drawn states). Semantic landmarks, strict heading order, `:focus-visible` rings on everything interactive, ≥44px touch targets, dark-mode token flip via `prefers-color-scheme`.
